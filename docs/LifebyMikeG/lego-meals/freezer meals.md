**Proteins**

1. **Turkey Chili**   
* **32 oz ground turkey**  
* **1 large onion (or two medium)**  
* **1 large green pepper (or two medium)**  
* **3-4 cloves of garlics**  
* **Pinch of oregano**  
* **32 oz tomato sauce**  
* **16 oz Kettle\&Fire chicken bone broth**  
* **6 dried chillis (guajillo, cascabelle, arbol, and ancho) Sweeter chilis can be used if less spice is desired. Jalapenos will work fine too.**   
1. In a pan on medium high heat, cook the ground turkey in olive oil or the fat of your choice. I would prefer dark meat since there’s much more fat, but I couldn't find any so I used white meat and added a bit more oil so it fries up nicely.   
2. Continue to cook the turkey until all the water evaporates and it begins to brown in the oil.   
3. In the meantime, chop your onion, garlic, and pepper. Prepare your dried chilis by deseeding and removing any stems.   
4. In a separate pan, toast your deseeded chilis until fragrant, about 5 minutes.  
5. Once you’ve removed the cooked and browned turkey from the pan and put it to the side in a bowl. Add your aromatics to the pan and cook until soft and the onions are translucent.   
6. Deglaze the pan with 32 oz of tomato sauce, then add a box of Kettle\&Fire chicken bone broth and bring everything to a boil.  
7. Tear up two corn tortillas and add them into the pot. This will provide a thickness to the chili without having to add any flour. Let cook for 5 minutes until the tortillas break down and then using a hand blender, blend the tortillas into the chili.  
8. Once blended, you can add back your turkey  and cook down again for another 5 minutes until super thick. You can now add the dried oregano.  
9. Don’t add beans before freezing\! You can add beans later for a heartier chilli or leave them out for an easy flavorful taco filling.   
10. Place in your molds and freeze overnight\!

**2\. Salsa Verde Braised Chicken** 

* **4 bone in skin on chicken thighs.**  
* **10 tomatillos**  
* **1 small onion, diced**   
* **1 clove of garlic**  
* **1 deseeded jalapeno**   
* **Handful of mint**  
* **Large handful of cilantro (about double the mint)**  
* **1 lime**  
* **2 TBSPN salt**

1. To a large pan, add a high heat frying oil on medium high heat. Season your chicken with salt and pepper then add to the pan skin side down.  
2. Sear for about 5 minutes on medium heat to gently brown and crisp the skin.   
3. While the chicken is frying, to a blender add your tomatillos, onion, garlic, deseeded jalapeno, mint, cilantro, the juice of one lime, and salt. Blend until very smooth so there are no chunks. Taste for salt and flavor, if you need a little more acidity you can add a splash of apple cider or white vinegar.   
4. Flip your chicken to brown on the other side. It’s not necessary to cook all the way through during this step since we are going to braise it.   
5. Once the second side is nicely golden brown, flip so the chicken is skin side up. Pour the salsa into the pan until it is just covering the chicken. Lower the heat and let simmer for 20-30 minutes or until the chicken is tender and falling off the bone.  
6. You can enjoy the chicken as is, or pull off the bone before eating or letting cool. Finish with another taste for salt and acidity.   
7. Place in your molds and freeze overnight\!

**3\. Tofu chorizo** 

* **2 packs of firm tofu**  
* **1 TBSP coconut oil**  
* **1 medium onion**   
* **1 chipotle pepper \+ 2 TBSPN of adobo sauce**  
* **4 cloves garlic**  
* **1 TBSP dried chili seasoning**  
* **1 TBSP soy sauce**  
* **1 TBSP maple syrup**  
* **Salt to taste**

1. Start with coconut oil in the pan at medium high heat. Finely dice and saute a whole onion in the pan, adding the sliced garlic once the onions are slightly caramelized and brown.  
2. Remove any excess water from the tofu packs, then add to the pan. No need to press or squeeze the tofu, the water will cook out in the pan.   
3. Crumble up into pieces into a ground meat texture to get it started then cook out most of the liquid. Once the liquid is cooked out, about 7-10 minutes, you can start adding your seasonings. Once the water is removed, it will start to fry up in the oil added at the beginning.   
4. Add all of your dried seasonings first, then the chipotle and adobo, soy, and maple syrup.   
5. Continue cooking until most of this added liquid also evaporates, about 5 minutes. Depending on how crispy and dry you prefer the tofu, you can cook longer 5-10 minutes more.   
6. Place in your molds and freeze overnight\!

**Veggie Side Dishes** 

**Braised Napa Cabbage**

* **Two medium napa cabbages (you can use any cabbage you want\!)**  
* **6 garlic cloves**  
* **8 oz Kette\&Fire Chicken bone broth**  
* **3 knobs of ginger**  
* **1 small and deseeded jalapeno**  
* **1 TBSP of ghee**  
    
1. Clean and quarter your cabbage.   
2. Turn your pan to medium high heat and add your choice of high heat tolerant oil. Place the cabbage quarters in the pan to sear, browning it on both sides, about 2-3 minutes each.   
3. Remove the browned cabbage from the pan and put to the side.   
4. Add all your aromatics, the garlic, ginger, and deseeded jalapeno into the pan with a TBPS of ghee.   
5. Once the aromatics are soft and fragrant, you can add the cabbage back into the pan.   
6. Add 8 oz of Kettle\&Fire chicken bone broth and a cup of water.  
7. Cover the cabbage and bring to a boil, then let reduce uncovered for 5 minutes.  
8. The longer you braise, the softer the cabbage will be, so texture and liquid level is up to you. Less time may have more water content, but will have a crunchier texture.   
9. Place in your molds and freeze overnight\!

**Sweet Potato plantain mash** 

* **3 medium sweet potatoes**  
* **3 ripe sweet yellow plantains**  
* **2 TBSP grapeseed oil**  
* **2 TBSP salt**

1. Chop sweet potatoes into large chunks, and the plantains into rounds, slightly bigger than the sweet potatoes since they roast slightly quicker.   
2. Season the veggies with grapeseed oil and salt.  
3. Place into the air fryer and roast for 20 minutes at 450 degrees.  
4. Take the tray out to flip them then roast for another 20 minutes.   
5. Remove from the air fryer and place into a bowl. Using a potato smasher, mash the veg into your preferred texture. Mash longer for a smoother mashed potato like texture or leave chunkier for more bite.   
6. Pour into molds and freeze at least overnight. 

**Fajita Mix** 

* **2 Red peppers**  
* **2 poblano peppers**  
* **1 large red onion**  
* **2 ears of corn**

1. Coat your whole veggies in a high heat oil and season well with salt.  
2. Preheat your grill to high heat, about 450 degrees, then place all your veggies on to roast. If you don’t have a grill, you can easily do this in a regular oven.   
3. Grill until soft and the veggies show slight char or grill marks and the skin begins to lift off.  
4. Once cooked to your preference, remove from the heat and let cool completely.   
5. Optionally remove skin from peppers.  
6. Slice peppers into medium bit sized pieces and remove corn kernels from the cob. Slice the red onion into thin strips.   
7. Mix the veg together in a bowl and taste for seasoning.  
8. Place in your molds and freeze overnight\!

**Roasted Eggplant**

* **Two large eggplants**  
* **2 cups cooked chickpeas**  
* **1 TSP cumin**  
* **1 TSP smoked paprika**  
* **⅓ cup tahini**  
* **Salt** 

1. On the grill or in the oven, roast your eggplants very well until fully cooked inside and the skin has separated.   
2. Place cooked eggplant in a bowl and cover with plastic wrap to steam and allow the skin to fully separate from the meat.   
3. Once cooled, remove the inside of the eggplant and place into the food processor with the chickpeas, cumin, tahini, paprika, and salt.  
4. Pulse a few times, enough to blend together but maintaining a chunky texture. If you prefer it smoother, just keep pulsing\!  
5. Finish with a little squeeze of olive oil and salt to taste.  
6. Place in your molds and freeze overnight.

**Starches** 

**Cilantro Rice** 

* **2 cups of Jasmine or Basmati rice**  
* **1 cup chopped cilantro**  
* **1 lime**  
* **1 cup Kettle\&Fire Chicken Broth**  
* **2 cups water**

1. Measure your rice then rinse well until the water runs clear.   
2. Add your rice, water, and Kettle\&Fire broth to the rice cooker and cook on the regular white rice setting.   
3. Once fully cooked, remove the rice from the rice cooker and mix to let cool slightly. You don’t want it so hot that it will cook the cilantro.  
4. After cooling, add your fresh cilantro and lime juice and mix until incorporated.   
5. Add to your molds and place in the freezer overnight.

**Black Beans** 

* **12 oz dried black beans**  
* **1 onion**  
* **2 cloves garlic**  
* **1 knob ginger**  
* **1 jalapeno, deseeded**

1. Add your beans and aromatics to a pressure cooker and cover completely with water.   
2. Cook for 18 minutes or until fork tender. Don’t season until after cooking to help control salt levels.   
3. Once fully cooked, release the steam from your pressure cooker.   
4. Add salt and pepper to taste then blend slightly with a hand blender.  
5. Depending on preference, you can blend now before freezing or keep the beans whole for more flexibility in the future\!   
6. If the beans seem to thin, just pop back onto the burner and reduce slightly until it is your preferred thickness.   
7. Add to your molds and place into the freezer overnight. 

**Farro “arroz rojo”** 

* **2 cups farro**  
* **4 cups of Kettle\&Fire Chicken Broth**  
* **3 medium tomatoes**  
* **1 small yellow onion**  
* **3 cloves garlic**  
* **2 TBSP olive oil**

1. Add oil to your pan then toast the farro on medium low heat for about 5 minutes.  
2. While the farro is toasting, add the onion, garlic, and tomatoes to a blender, and blend until very smooth.   
3. Once the farro is golden brown and fragrant from toasting, pour in your tomato mixture as well as a cup of water and a cup of Kettle\&Fire broth. Add your de-seeded jalapeno.   
4. Cook for 15-20 minutes or until the farro has popped open and become tender. If there is any excess liquid but the farro is fully cooked, simply strain before freezing. Since we are freezing here, it’s better to under cook than to over cook\! Farro is hearty and can handle the reheating so don’t be afraid to maintain some texture.   
5. Place in your molds and freeze overnight\!

**Sauces** 

**Smokey Pepper BBQ Sauce**

* **1 medium onion**  
* **2 cloves garlic**  
* **1/4 cup molasses**  
* **2 cups ketchup**  
* **1 Ancho chili**  
* **1 Cascabel chili**   
* **2 Arbor chilis**  
* **1/2 cup vinegar**  
* **1/2-1 cup Kettle\&Fire Broth**  
* **Salt to taste** 

1. Finely chop your onions and garlic, then add to a preheated pan with oil on medium high heat.   
2. Cook until translucent, then add your dried and deseeded chilis to toast slightly.   
3. Once the chilis are slightly fried and fragrant, add your ketchup, molasses, and vinegar and mix everything together.  
4. Let this mixture reduce on the stove for about 5- 10 minutes, making sure to stir occasionally to prevent any sticking or burning.   
5. Turn off the heat and let cool slightly, then move the mixture over to a blender and blend on high until super smooth.   
6. If your mixture is too thick, slowly add room temperature Kettle\&Fire broth to loosen to your preferred pouring texture.   
7. Pop into a squeeze bottle and enjoy\!

**Cilantro “Crema”**

* **1 egg yolk**  
* **1 large egg**  
* **2 TBSP vinegar**  
* **1 TBSP mustard**   
* **3 cloves garlic**  
* **2 cups avocado oil**  
* **1 cup cilantro leaves**   
* **2 TBSP salt**

1. To a blender or a food processor, add your egg yolk, egg, vinegar, mustard, garlic, and salt.   
2. Blend this mixture well until super smooth and there are no large pieces of garlic left.   
3. Keep the blender or processor on, then very slowly begin to add your oil. We are essentially making a mayonnaise here, slowly emulsifying the oil into our mixture.   
4. Once the mixture begins to thicken up, you can add the oil more quickly.  
5. Once fully emulsified, remove about half the mayonnaise mixture from the blender.  
6. Add in your cilantro and blend on high until the cilantro breaks down into very small pieces and the sauce appears green.  
7.  Add back in the rest of your mayo mixture and blend until fully emulsified.   
8. Pop into a squeeze bottle and enjoy\!
